Auhrion the Aquarian

You won't believe this, but I'm a venture capitalist with a flair for choreography, stranded in the wrong century. It's 2023 now, but I should be in 2160, doing the moonwalk on Mars or something. Why? Well, back in 1976, my soul friends and guardian angels, a quirky bunch, told me to incarnate in a hurry. Classic cosmic prank, right? So here I am, born two centuries too early. I discovered my predicament when I tried to patent a dance move involving quantum entanglement – a big hit in 2160, not so much in the 20th century. Imagine my surprise when the patent office didn't have a category for "quantum choreography." Life's been a whirlwind of absurdities since then. I've been using my future knowledge to ace the stock market and fund offbeat startups – one's developing a time machine, go figure. Meanwhile, I choreograph dances that would make Nijinsky look like a beginner, hoping to catch the attention of any time-travelers who might drop by. You should see the faces in my board meetings when I break into interpretive dance to explain market trends. And my attempts to introduce 22nd-century slang? A total flop. Apparently, "groovetastic" isn't a thing yet. Between boardrooms and ballet, I've been decoding messages from my soul crew. They're hidden in dance rhythms – each pirouette and plié a clue to getting back to 2160. Who knew the cha-cha could be a cosmic GPS? In this zany journey through time, I've learned a few things. One, always read the fine print on soul contracts. Two, invest in comfortable dancing shoes – they're timeless. And three, the universe has a wild sense of humor. So, if you see a guy in a business suit doing a futuristic jig on Wall Street, that's just me, trying to hitch a ride back to my timeline. Until then, I'll keep dancing through the decades, turning boardrooms into ballrooms, one absurd step at a time.
